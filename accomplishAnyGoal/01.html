Story starts from here..


Have you ever set a goal and created a detailed to-do list but failed to accomplish it? I bet everyone did it at some point of their life. Accomplish any goal, including mastering an instrument, passing an exam or getting fit is not simply a to-do list. Instead, the recipe to success has many other ingredients: mentorship, proper planning, motivation, goal setting, progress tracking, consistency, work ethics etc. Most apps focus on one piece of puzzle, but fail to include others. We want to know if we can leverage technology to support whoever wants to accomplish a long-term goal. Therefore, we propose our design question as the following:

“How to use interactive technology to help support users reach their long-term goal?”



Who are involved


General Users

People who are seeking for tools that help to accomplish their long term goals.

Mentors

People who are experienced and knowledgeable in one field and are willing to mentor others.

Businesses

Books, apps and training business that help users to improve specific skillsets.

Research




Before we jump into the desiging phase, we need to figure out what users need, and what already exists on the market. So we started off our researching questions as the following:

  • What make people fail a long-term goal?
  • What are helpful to help people to reach a long-term goal?
  • What areas do people think they need to get better when working toward a goal?

We interviewed 5 people across different occupations to find out what's the pain point of their jouney.

We also conducted competitive analysis and searched for the related apps that exist on the market. Most apps focus on:

  • Visualization of the progress (chart, check boxes and calendar)
  • Set daily reminder
  • Divide big goals into smaller tasks

Here are a few researches and books that provide evidence supporting the listed features: "5 Facts About Goal Setting" "Organize Your Life and Goals"

Key Findings & Insights


(1) People need different help in different stages of their journey to succeed

Focusing on one aspect of accomplishing a long term goal in the app could be a good choice, which many app developer have already made. For example, many apps focus on forming a good habit. We decided to have a variety of tools for user to choose from . Therefore, the design question becomes how to make our product or services seamless, simple but not overwhelming for users to use.

(2) The social components seem to be missing in most of apps/websties.

According to situated learning theories, people learn by socializing with others. ZPD theory (Zone of Proximal Development) showed that people can do more with the help of an experienced pioneer or peer. Therefore, another design question is how can we bring the social elements into our product, making sure it helps our users move toward their goals.

(3) Simplicity, fun, visually appealing, sense of progress are the key words.

Through interviews, "simplicity", "fun", and "visually appealing" are extremely common words that users describe that appeals them the most to an application. This indicated to us that design should be focus on the ease of human-computer interaction. Progress should be visual presented, but also in a motivating way. After all, working towards a main goal is already complicated, so why should users need to spend the extra effort figuring out how to use our product?

Prototyping & Wire Frame



According to our research, our team tried to come up with something that both meet user needs, but also fun to engage with. We think we could apply some gamification in our design so user can see how their daily actions contributing to an over-arching long-term goal. Through team brainstorm and discussions, as shown on the left side, we came up with more than 10 possible design solutions. Since we are also the web developer for this project and we are on a tight timeline, we had to give up some cool ideas and turned to more simple and realistic ones that can do the job but also meet the timeline.

Here are the key elements we decided that have to be incorportaed into our design:

  • Start with defining proper goal
  • Divide the goals in to milestone or stepstones
  • Motivation
  • Building a community

Reflection



  • More time should be invested in the research phase.

    When we went into the brain storming and designing phase, we encounter occasions that design decisions couldn't be made firmly due to the lack of understanding of user behaviors. Then we realised we actually need to invest more time and be more strategic in the research phase. Lessons are learned.

  • Understand who is the product desgined for and what's the purpose of this products.

    During user testing, we had a chance to hear different voices and opinions. For example, one user would like to use this tool as a to-do list, and the goals she put in the system were small items and she hoped she could see all the tasks on the screen. We could have easily decided that we should build a feature to display all the tasks, but do we really need to? Does that really help people focus on their long term goals? I still don't have an answer yet. It's really tempting to add more and more into our product, but I am a firm believer of "less is more". I am glad that me and my teammates are always on the same channel and we have to remind us "Keep it simple" and "What problem did we try to solve".
